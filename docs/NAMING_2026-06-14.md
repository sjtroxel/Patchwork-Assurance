Why it won:
- **Patchwork** survived every pass (the industry's own word for the 50-state AI-law landscape;

  legal check for AI compliance *across the states* for businesses."
- **Assurance** = the professional compliance-verification term; B2B and serious, which fits the

  differentiates and reads less like a student project.

## The convention

Two words, matching the existing portfolio (Heritage Odyssey, Wildlife Sentinel, Poster Pilot,
Asteroid Bonanza, ChronoQuizzr). The good ones work by *friction*: a plain domain noun married to
a second noun that reframes it. One-word names felt too simple for the complexity this app means
to hold.

## Hard constraints (learned from rejected candidates)

- **No source-narrow legal words** (e.g. "Statute"). Case law, regulations, and federal action all
  need to fit, so the legal word must mean *all* law, not one kind. This ruled out Statute Loom,
  Statute Atlas.
- **No star imagery.** "Lodestar" reads as "Lone Star" to many, implying a Texas-specific app.
  Ruled out Lex Lodestar (otherwise liked).
- **No royal connotations.** Ruled out "Sovereign."
- **No competitor / AI-product names or echoes** — not Codex, not Claude, not Antigravity, not
  Gemini/Copilot/GPT. Avoid inviting that association at all. Ruled out Sovereign Codex.
- **Must be genuinely catchy**, not just technically apt.

## Words that keep surviving

- **Lex** (Latin, law in general — broad enough to cover case law and regs). Caveat: legaltech is
  saturated with "Lex-" names because of LexisNexis, so a Lex-prefix can read slightly generic.
- **Patchwork** (the industry's own word for the 50-state AI-law landscape; source-neutral;
  liked). Note: "Lex Patchwork" specifically was rejected — two abstract nouns with no rhythm.
