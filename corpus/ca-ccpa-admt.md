# California — CCPA Automated Decisionmaking Technology (ADMT) Regulations
## California Code of Regulations, Title 11, Div. 6, Chapter 1 (California Privacy Protection Agency / CCPA)
### 11 CCR §§ 7001, 7150, 7200, 7220, 7221, 7222 (CCPA Updates, Cyber, Risk, ADMT, and Insurance Regulations) (Excerpt)
*Source: California Privacy Protection Agency, Text of Regulations (approved text) — https://cppa.ca.gov/regulations/pdf/ccpa_updates_cyber_risk_admt_appr_text.pdf*
*Adopted by the CPPA Board July 24, 2025; approved by the Office of Administrative Law and filed with the Secretary of State September 22, 2025. Effective January 1, 2026; ADMT (this Article) compliance required by January 1, 2027.*

> Excerpt note: these are the California Privacy Protection Agency's CCPA regulations governing
> automated decisionmaking technology (ADMT). This is a **consumer-privacy** regime — pre-use notice,
> opt-out, access, and a risk-assessment-before-use duty — distinct from California's FEHA employment
> ADS regulations (`ca-feha-ads`), which impose discrimination liability. Reproduced below are the
> ADMT-for-significant-decisions provisions: the definitions (§ 7001), the risk-assessment trigger
> (§ 7150), the scope rule (§ 7200), and the consumer-facing duties (§§ 7220–7222). The full
> regulations also cover cybersecurity audits, broader risk assessments, and insurance companies, not
> reproduced here.

## § 7001. Definitions. (Excerpt: ADMT and significant decision)

**(e) "Automated decisionmaking technology" or "ADMT"** means any technology that processes personal
information and uses computation to replace human decisionmaking or substantially replace human
decisionmaking.

(1) For purposes of this definition, to "substantially replace human decisionmaking" means a business
uses the technology's output to make a decision without human involvement. Human involvement requires the
human reviewer to:

(A) Know how to interpret and use the technology's output to make the decision;

(B) Review and analyze the output of the technology, and any other information that is relevant to make
or change the decision; and

(C) Have the authority to make or change the decision based on their analysis in subsection (B).

(2) ADMT includes profiling that replaces human decisionmaking or substantially replaces human
decisionmaking.

(3) ADMT does not include web hosting, domain registration, networking, caching, website-loading, data
storage, firewalls, anti-virus, anti-malware, spam- and robocall-filtering, spellchecking, calculators,
databases, and spreadsheets, provided that they do not replace human decisionmaking.

**(ddd) "Significant decision"** means a decision that results in the provision or denial of financial or
lending services, housing, education enrollment or opportunities, employment or independent contracting
opportunities or compensation, or healthcare services. For purposes of this definition, "financial or
lending services" means the extension of credit or a loan, transmitting or exchanging funds, the
provision of deposit or checking accounts, check cashing, or installment payment plans; and "housing"
means any building, structure, or portion thereof that is used or occupied as, or designed, arranged, or
intended to be used or occupied as, a home, residence, or sleeping place by one or more consumers.

## § 7150. When a Business Must Conduct a Risk Assessment. (Excerpt)

(a) Every business whose processing of consumers' personal information presents significant risk to
consumers' privacy as set forth in subsection (b) must conduct a risk assessment before initiating that
processing.

(b) Each of the following processing activities presents significant risk to consumers' privacy:

(3) Using ADMT for a significant decision concerning a consumer.

---

## § 7200. When a Business's Use of Automated Decisionmaking Technology is Subject to the Requirements of This Article.

(a) A business that uses ADMT to make a significant decision concerning a consumer must comply with the
requirements of this Article.

(b) A business that uses ADMT for a significant decision prior to January 1, 2027, must be in compliance
with the requirements of this Article no later than January 1, 2027. A business that uses ADMT on or
after January 1, 2027, must be in compliance with the requirements of this Article any time it is using
ADMT for a significant decision.

---

## § 7220. Pre-use Notice Requirements. (Excerpt)

(a) A business that uses ADMT as set forth in section 7200, subsection (a), must provide consumers with a
Pre-use Notice. The Pre-use Notice must inform consumers about the business's use of ADMT and consumers'
rights to opt-out of ADMT and to access ADMT, as set forth in this section.

---

## § 7221. Requests to Opt-Out of ADMT. (Excerpt)

(a) A business must provide consumers with the ability to opt-out of the use of ADMT to make a
significant decision concerning the consumer, except as set forth in subsection (b).

(b) A business is not required to provide consumers with the ability to opt-out of a business's use of
ADMT to make a significant decision in certain enumerated circumstances (for example, where the ADMT is
necessary to provide a good or service the consumer requested and a human-appeal alternative is offered).

---

## § 7222. Requests to Access ADMT. (Excerpt)

(a) A business that uses ADMT to make a significant decision must provide a consumer with information
about this use when responding to a consumer's request to access ADMT.

(b) When responding to a consumer's request to access ADMT, a business must provide plain language
explanations of, among other things, the specific purpose for which the business used ADMT with respect
to the consumer, the output of the ADMT with respect to the consumer, and how the business used that
output to make the significant decision.
